Who I am

I am a earth system modeler and a data enthusiast. I build mathematical and statistical methods to solve problems in nature and the society. I am passionate about discovering the world with the power of data.

2002

College Life

I moved out of my hometown and started my journey as a college freshman.

2006

Sweden

I became a master student in Lund University to study Environment science and Sustainability development. There I met friends from all over the world and we were debating over and over about the big, and perhaps the oldest, issues of the human society. We strived to understand and solve the problems of the wolrd's development with policical and soitial means. We learned from each other about the country, the language, the culture, the beauty the challages of their society.

2008

Graduation and the post-graduation chaos

In June 2006, with the fresh master's degree and the excitement of getting into the real world, I was ready for any challage above me.I moved to Stockholm and joined a business events company as a sales and marketing trainee. I learned about corporates and business and the ways of communication.

2009

Back to academia

After two years of thinking over social sicence and six months of living in industry, I decided to go back to natural science and find out more about climate change. At the time I believed that climate change was the biggest problem the human beings were facing and that the ultimate solution lied in science. I landed in Canada in a beautiful October afternoon and started the life as a phd candidate. I was fulfilled everyday with math, physics, chemistry, biology and most of all programming.

2012

Germany ,where philosophy meets classical music

I moved to Germany to complete my PhD by chance and it turned out be a once-in-a-lifetime experience. While finishing my work on climate change and modeling, I immersed myself in the greatest classical music alive and the everlasting spirit of philosophy in this old country.

2014

Back to Canada and carry on

Fairwell to Europe and I am back in Canada. I was granted for a post-doc fellowship to expand my research in Envirionment Canada. I was exicted to be part of an incrediable team that builds the global climate model for the United Nations IPCC reports. I learned all kinds of techniques in programming for complex problems and dealing with massive data from the saterlites and individual campaign.

2015

Becoming a data scientist

I met data science while learning a new python library and I wished it had happened earlier. The magic of machine learning solves analytical problems from a brand new perspective and the self-evolving nature of the artificial intelligence made it possible to predict the future much more precisely than human minds have ever achieved. I just know that I have to do this.I started to search for everything about data science and every possible way to learn it. I studied the open courses from Harvard and Stanford and build up the basic skillkit and understandings of the fundemental statistics and the math theories. I also found the case-driving program from MIT online and practiced through all the cases. As I enjoyed so much working with the real world problems, I participated in a Kaggle competition. It was an interesting problem predicting the most popular blog on New Year's times, and working with natural language was both fun and challenging. I am still learning new skills, for example big data related tools, data vitualization tools d3.js and many more on the list...
